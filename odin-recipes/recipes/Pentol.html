Pentol

Description

Pentol is a popular and affordable street snack from Indonesia, particularly common in areas like East Java, Central Java, and Yogyakarta . It is a type of savory meatball, but unlike traditional bakso, pentol typically contains less meat and is often made primarily from flour or starch, giving it a chewy, bouncy texture

These simple, filling snacks are versatile and can be served in many ways. They are commonly enjoyed with flavorful sauces like peanut sauce, sweet soy sauce, tomato sauce, or spicy sambal . Pentol sellers are a common sight, selling their wares from carts, motorbikes, or bicycles, often near schools, markets, and other crowded places . For extra variety, you can also find them with creative fillings like quail eggs or as part of heartier dishes, such as in a spicy "mercon" (firecracker) stew

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast fillet (dada ayam fillet)
  • Garlic (bawang putih)
  • Shallots (bawang merah)
  • Eggs (telur ayam)
  • Tapioca flour (tepung tapioka)
  • All-purpose flour (tepung terigu)
  • Rice flour (tepung beras) — optional, for flour-based version
  • Soy sauce (kecap asin)
  • Spring onions (daun bawang)
  • Salt (garam)
  • Sugar (gula pasir)
  • Chicken powder seasoning (kaldu ayam bubuk)
  • Pepper (merica)
  • MSG or flavor enhancer (penyedap rasa) — optional
  • Fried peanuts (kacang tanah goreng) — for sauce
  • Red chili (cabai merah keriting) — for sauce
  • Bird's eye chili (cabai rawit merah) — for sauce
  • Candlenuts (kemiri) — for sauce
  • Lime leaves (daun jeruk) — for sauce
  • Sweet soy sauce (kecap manis) — for sauce
  • Palm sugar (gula merah) — for sauce
  • Water (air) — for sauce and boiling

Steps

  1. Blend chicken, garlic, shallots, and eggs until smooth.
  2. Mix blended mixture with tapioca flour, all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, pepper, and chicken seasoning in a bowl.
  3. Add spring onions and soy sauce, then stir into a stiff dough.
  4. Wet your hands, then roll dough into small balls.
  5. Boil a pot of water until rolling.
  6. Drop balls in and cook until they float (3–5 minutes).
  7. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
  8. Fry or roast peanuts, chilies, shallots, garlic, and candlenuts until fragrant.
  9. Blend fried ingredients with lime leaves, palm sugar, salt, and water until smooth.
  10. Stir in sweet soy sauce to taste.
  11. Arrange pentol on a plate.
  12. Pour sauce over or serve on the side.
  13. Enjoy warm.
