PIZZA

Description

Pizza is a globally beloved baked dish that typically consists of a round, flattened base of yeasted wheat dough topped with a savory blend of tomato sauce, melted cheese (most famously mozzarella), and a variety of other ingredients, which is then cooked at high temperatures, traditionally in a wood-fired oven. While its modern, iconic form—the *Pizza Margherita* with its red sauce, white cheese, and green basil representing the Italian flag—originated in Naples, Italy, pizza has evolved into a highly versatile culinary canvas. Today, it ranges from the classic, thin-crust Neapolitan with simple, high-quality toppings to the thick, deep-dish Chicago style or the chewy, oversized New York slice, and can feature everything from pepperoni and vegetables to exotic gourmet toppings like figs or prosciutto, making it a universally customizable comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Yeast
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Olive oil
  • Tomatoes (crushed or whole)
  • Salt
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Extra virgin olive oil (for drizzling)
  • Salt and black pepper>

Steps

  1. Mix flour, yeast, salt, water, and oil into a dough.
  2. Knead until smooth.
  3. Let it rise for 1–2 hours.
  4. Preheat oven to its highest setting.
  5. Flatten dough into a round circle.
  6. Spread tomato sauce over the base.
  7. Scatter mozzarella and basil on top.
  8. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle salt/pepper.
  9. Bake for 10–15 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  10. Slice and eat.
