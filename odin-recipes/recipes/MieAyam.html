Mie Ayam

Description

mie ayam, also known as mie ayam or bakmi ayam, is a beloved Indonesian noodle dish consisting of seasoned yellow wheat noodles generously topped with savory diced chicken . It is a popular Chinese-Indonesian dish, a testament to its culinary roots in Chinese cuisine , and is a ubiquitous sight across the country—served by traveling street vendors, at humble roadside warungs, and in established restaurants

The dish is made by first boiling the wheat noodles until they are perfectly "al dente" and then tossing them in a flavorful mixture of oil, soy sauce, and garlic, which coats the noodles and prevents them from sticking together . The chicken topping is prepared separately, diced and cooked with soy sauce, garlic, and other seasonings, sometimes with the addition of mushrooms . This richly seasoned chicken is then placed atop the noodles, and the dish is finished with a sprinkle of chopped spring onions

mie ayam is almost always accompanied by a separate, clear chicken broth . It is often served with blanched Chinese cabbage and can be further enhanced with wonton (pangsit), either crispy fried or floating in the soup, and occasionally with bakso (meatballs) . While the original Chinese version may use pork, the Indonesian mie ayam commonly uses halal chicken fat or vegetable oil . A variety of additional condiments, such as sambal, pickled cucumber, and fried shallots (bawang goreng), are often available to customize each bowl

Ingredients

  • Yellow wheat noodles (mie basah or mie kering)
  • Chicken breast or thigh (dada atau paha ayam)
  • Garlic (bawang putih)
  • Shallots (bawang merah)
  • Soy sauce (kecap asin)
  • Sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • Oyster sauce (saos tiram) — optional
  • Sesame oil (minyak wijen)
  • Vegetable oil (minyak goreng)
  • Salt (garam)
  • Sugar (gula pasir)
  • White pepper (merica putih)
  • Chicken powder seasoning (kaldu ayam bubuk)
  • Mushrooms (jamur) — optional
  • Spring onions (daun bawang)
  • Chinese cabbage (sawi hijau or caisim)
  • Chicken bones (tulang ayam) — for broth
  • Water (air) — for boiling and broth
  • Fried shallots (bawang goreng) — for garnish
  • Wonton / pangsit (optional)
  • Bakso / meatballs (optional)
  • Sambal (chili sauce) — for serving
  • Pickled cucumber (acar timun) — for serving

Steps

  1. Boil chicken bones in water to make clear broth, season with salt, pepper, and chicken powder, then set aside.
  2. Dice chicken meat into small cubes.
  3. Finely chop garlic and shallots.
  4. Heat oil in a pan, sauté garlic and shallots until fragrant.
  5. Add diced chicken, cook until it changes color.
  6. Add soy sauce, sweet soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, salt, sugar, and pepper.
  7. Stir well and cook until chicken is fully cooked and sauce thickens, set aside.
  8. Boil yellow wheat noodles in a pot of hot water until al dente.
  9. Blanch Chinese cabbage in the same boiling water briefly, then drain.
  10. In a bowl, mix a little oil, soy sauce, and sesame oil as a noodle coating base.
  11. Drain cooked noodles, then toss them in the bowl with the coating mixture.
  12. Place coated noodles in a serving bowl.
  13. Top with the cooked chicken mixture.
  14. Garnish with chopped spring onions and fried shallots.
  15. Pour the hot broth into a separate small bowl (to serve on the side).
  16. Add wonton or bakso into the broth if using.
  17. Serve noodles with broth on the side, along with sambal and pickled cucumber.
  18. Enjoy while warm.
